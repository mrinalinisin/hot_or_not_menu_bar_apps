“I have seen enough pomodoro, screenshotting, window manager, and clipboard apps for life.”

— you, and the numbers agree: 148 apps (10.3% of the entire catalogue) are one of those four things. Timers of some kind — pomodoro plus plain timers and break-nudgers — are the single most crowded theme at 133 apps (~9%).

The flip side: 58% don’t fit any obvious bucket — a genuinely long tail. The catalogue only feels repetitive because a dozen crowded categories sit on top of a very diverse base.

The smoking gun 🔍

Two sentences appear word-for-word across multiple different apps — a strong tell that some blurbs are auto-generated from a template:

“This comprehensive tool helps you stay organized and efficient throughout your workday without distractions or complications.”

— appears verbatim in 10 different apps

“This streamlines your workflow significantly.”

— verbatim in 3.

The takeaway

The catalogue leans hard on a small vocabulary: lightweight / simple / clean / fast apps that let you / help you do things instantly and quietly, right from your menu bar, without distractions.
